Design Section 4: Screens, Route Identity, Replay Drama, Difficulty

Title/death/victory overhaul, waypoint visual identity, post-run storytelling, and <10% win rate tuning.

Screen Overhauls — Title / Death / Victory

Title Screen — "Expedition Briefing"

Rebuilds the boot sequence as a layered cinematic experience over the mountain scene.

  1. Mountain scene fades in first — dawn sky, distant ridges emerge from black over 2s. The player sees the mountain before anything else.
  2. Terminal boot overlay — existing boot lines appear over the scene in a floating console panel. System check flavor text. Keep the current typewriter pacing but tighten it (200ms per line instead of 280ms).
  3. Route drawing sequence — after boot completes, a thin line traces the Ao Tai route across the mountain silhouette (SVG path animation, 2s). Waypoint markers appear as the line reaches each point. The route glows briefly then dims to tactical green.
  4. Title reveal — "AO TAI CYBER-HIKE" appears with the mountain behind it. Chinese subtitle. Route statistics: "80km · 13 waypoints · 3767m summit · <10% survival rate".
  5. Expedition dossier lines — brief rotating flavor text below title: weather warnings, route ban notices, missing hiker reports. Cycles every 4s. Sets the danger tone.
  6. Prompt — "PRESS ENTER TO BEGIN EXPEDITION" with restrained amber pulse. Mountain scene continues with subtle cloud/atmospheric movement behind everything.

Key change: The mountain is visible throughout the title. The terminal boot happens over the mountain, not instead of it. The player's first impression is the mountain itself.

Death Screen — "Signal Lost"

Death is a two-phase dramatic sequence, replacing the current dying flash + static overlay.

Phase 1: The Moment (2s)

  • Mountain scene freezes. All weather particles halt mid-frame.
  • Cause of death text appears center-screen in large hazard red: "HYPOTHERMIA" / "FALL" / "EXHAUSTION" / etc.
  • Scene rapidly desaturates to near-monochrome.
  • Hiker figure flickers and goes dark.
  • Brief static burst (100ms) — then silence.

Phase 2: The Report (holds until restart)

  • Mountain scene remains frozen and desaturated in background — a memorial image of where you fell.
  • Floating "expedition report" panel appears center:
  • // SIGNAL LOST
    // LAST KNOWN POSITION: 金字塔 (PYRAMID PEAK) 3478m
    // CAUSE: HYPOTHERMIA
    // DAY 6 · HOUR 02:00 · BLIZZARD
    // DISTANCE: 42.3km / 80km
    // WAYPOINTS REACHED: 7 / 13
    // TURNS SURVIVED: 31
  • Below the report: the run summary / replay section (see Replay Drama below).
  • "RESTART MISSION" button with restrained pulse.

Key change: Death shows where you died on the actual mountain. The frozen scene with your last position visible is the epitaph. No generic static screen — it's your specific death on that specific ridge.

Victory Screen — "Summit Reached" / "Escape"

Summit Victory (Ending 2 — the rare one):

  • Mountain scene transitions to summit band profile. Sky clears (or holds storm if weather was bad — surviving a storm summit is even more dramatic).
  • Hiker figure plays summit pose — arms raised, brief golden glow.
  • If weather permits: dawn gold or clear blue sky breaks through. Light rays from behind the peak (CSS gradient animation). The sky opens.
  • If weather is terrible: the summit pose plays in the storm. No golden light. Just survival. Even more powerful.
  • Title: "SUMMIT REACHED" in earned green glow — the only time full bright green is used. text-shadow: 0 0 30px.
  • "拔仙台 · BAXIAN PLATFORM · 3767m" subtitle.
  • Expedition report with full route summary below.

Escape Victory (Ending 1):

  • Mountain scene transitions to forest approach band. Dawn or morning sky. Warmer, calmer.
  • Hiker walks back into the lower terrain. Scene feels like descent and relief.
  • Title: "ESCAPE" in amber. No celebration glow — survival, not triumph.
  • Tone: "You turned back. You lived. Most don't."

Route Identity — Waypoint Visual Differentiation

Tactical Map Upgrades

The tactical map stays isometric SVG but gains richer visual language:

  • Route line treatment: Line thickness and pulse rate scale with danger. Safe terrain = thin steady line. Dangerous terrain = thicker line with slow pulse. Extreme terrain = thick pulsing red-tinged line.
  • Traversed vs future: Traversed trail glows with earned tactical green. Future trail shown as dim dashed line with terrain-colored tint (green for forest, gray for rock, blue-white for exposed, red for storm ridge).
  • Waypoint markers: Current waypoint gets a breathing ring animation. Passed waypoints show solid diamond. Future waypoints show hollow diamond. Shelter waypoints get a subtle interior icon (tiny roof shape). Camp-available waypoints get a dot.
  • Terrain hazard zones: Subtle shaded regions on the map for high-danger terrain bands. Storm Ridge section gets a faint red overlay. Exposed Plateau gets a wind-streak overlay. These are always visible — tactical information, not reactive effects.
  • Lost state: Route line ahead dissolves. Waypoint markers blur. Search radius ring pulses. Hiker marker drifts off the trail line. The map itself becomes uncertain.
  • Point of no return: A subtle but permanent marker at waypoint 10. After passing it, the "DESCEND" action label changes. The map shows no return path beyond this point.

Replay Drama — Post-Run Storytelling

Run Summary System

Shown on the death/victory screen below the expedition report. Turns a failed run into a story worth retelling.

Components:

  • Route trace: A miniature version of the tactical map showing how far you got. Traversed path glows. Final position marked with an X (death) or star (summit). Clean, small, immediately readable.
  • Event timeline: 3-5 key moments from the run displayed as a compact timeline. Auto-selected by severity: worst weather, injuries, getting lost, critical vitals, key decisions. Format: "Day 3 · Blizzard struck at 梁1 · Day 4 · Fall at stone sea · Day 6 · Lost in whiteout".
  • Expedition epitaph: A single generated line summarizing the run. Template-based from run data: "Lost to hypothermia on the storm ridge after 6 days. The blizzard on Day 4 was the turning point." Uses existing fallback narrator template approach — no Ollama dependency.
  • Run codename: Auto-generated expedition name from the run characteristics. Template: "[Weather]-[Terrain]-[Fate]" combinations. Examples: "Operation Frozen Ridge," "The Stone Sea Retreat," "Whiteout Protocol." Adds replayability flavor.

Data source: The game log already records everything needed. The run summary component reads the log entries, filters for severity/type, and formats the timeline. No engine changes required — this is a pure presentation feature built on existing data.

Difficulty Retune — Target <10% Win Rate

Balance Strategy

Current calibration: ~15% AI summit rate from 220+ playtest games. Target: <10%.

Tuning levers (engine changes):

Lever Current Proposed Change Rationale
Weather escalation Day 4+ shift Day 3+ shift, stronger weighting toward snow/blizzard Earlier and harder weather pressure. The mountain gets hostile sooner.
Night travel Higher lost chance +15% energy drain at night, +25% lost chance, +10% fall risk Night should be genuinely terrifying to push through. Forces camp discipline.
Camp recovery 100% / 35% / 10% 85% / 30% / 8% Slightly reduce first-camp recovery. Every camp matters more.
Morale decay Per-action decrease Steeper decay in bad weather and at night. Isolation compounds. Morale collapse should be a real threat, not just a number that slowly ticks down.
Getting lost 6% base 8% base, with stronger weather/night modifiers Getting lost is the most dramatic mechanic. Slightly more common makes navigation a real concern.
Altitude drain Above 3000m Steeper O2 drain above 3500m. Energy drain compounds with altitude + bad weather. The final approach to the summit should feel punishing. Every step costs more.

Validation approach:

  • Use existing playtest bot scripts in scripts/
  • Run 200+ simulations with heuristic bot after tuning
  • Target: 5-9% summit rate (sweet spot for "possible but rare")
  • Verify that deaths feel attributable to decisions + conditions, not pure RNG
  • Ensure the median run reaches at least waypoint 5-7 (the game should be a journey, not instant death)
  • Document tuning changes and simulation results

Philosophy: The difficulty increase should come from environmental pressure (weather, night, altitude) and resource tension (camp recovery, morale), not from arbitrary damage spikes. The player should always understand why they died.
